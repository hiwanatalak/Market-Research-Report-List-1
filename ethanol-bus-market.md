<p><p>The ethanol bus market is categorized into two main types: first-generation and second-generation ethanol buses. First-generation ethanol buses utilize bioethanol produced from food crops like corn and sugarcane, consequently raising concerns about food supply and sustainability. In contrast, second-generation ethanol buses are powered by advanced bioethanol derived from non-food biomass, such as agricultural residues or waste materials, promoting environmental sustainability and reducing competition with food resources. Both aim to provide cleaner alternatives to traditional fossil fuel buses.</p></p>
